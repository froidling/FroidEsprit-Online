About Me

Hello again to those who wandered here. I gave you a brief intro on the mainpage, but here's a bit more.

I'm Ethan, a Vancouver-based finance student, ham radio operator, and software enthusiast.

I additionally have interests in virtual reality, space, photography, and vocal synthesis. I have a Meta Quest 3S and a copy of 'GUMI' Megpoid AI 2 for Synthesizer V Studio 2.

Among my weapons of choice, I have a Canon EOS Rebel T5i DSLR camera, still going strong, a Kobo Libra Colour, and a Quansheng UV-K5 handheld radio. My most recent additions to my collection are a half-assembled QRP Labs QMX+ and an RTL-SDR dongle.

I'm currently eyeing the AnyTone AT-D890UV handheld and the SlimeVR Butterfly trackers.

It should come as little surprise to you that I'm not the best web developer, or software dev in general. Or, at the very least, I'm not nearly as good as the average user of this site. That said, I do dabble. Not enough to consider myself fluent in any given programming language, and certainly not enough to make an app or program of any kind, but I dabble.

My main experience is with Kotlin. Not very much, and I can't keep up with the latest frameworks Google puts out for Android development, but I have a theoretical understanding of the language's funcitons. I've also dabbled in Python, and made some toys with it, including a text based calculator at some point a long time ago, a shame I never put it on GitHub before losing it.

Through my limited experience with it so far, I believe I can safely say I can't stand JavaScript.
